Modifications And Refunds

Modifying/Cancelling your booking

We recommend going to the airline's official site or app if you need to make changes to your reservation. You might also hire Airline Travel Support to take care of things for you.

Getting a refund

In the event of cancellation, you may be eligible for a full or partial refund. Depending on the kind of flight ticket you purchased, you will either get a full refund to your original payment method or flight points will be applied to your airline account. We strongly advise you to call Airline Travel Support, where you will be dealing with genuine professionals rather than customer service representatives. During our interactions with the airline, we will try our utmost to achieve a reimbursement.

Baggage Information

Most airlines, as you may know, offer a free hand baggage policy that allows travellers to bring a small briefcase, handbag, laptop, or backpack on board for free. For additional information on luggage policies, please contact our Airline Travel Support team.

Onboard Services

All airline passengers have access to complimentary onboard amenities such as meals and in-flight entertainment such as movies, magazines, and Wi-Fi. These onboard services may change from one airline to the next, so contact us as soon as possible to find out what will be available on your journey.

Flight Delay And Cancellation

A flight being cancelled or delayed is an uncommon occurrence. Airlines, on the other hand, may book you on the next available flight or accommodate you in another way in such instances. Even while this is exceedingly unlikely, it is not out of the realm of possibility. Most airlines will provide a full refund or some form of compensation to passengers who have been inconvenienced.

Boarding Process

Passengers are usually separated into groups depending on the number on their airline ticket. When boarding, keep alert for announcements and follow the instructions when your group is called for a seamless transfer from the airport to the plane.

Documentation Required

When you display your airline ticket, don't forget to carry your government-issued picture ID. It must be validated by airport security and the airline at the check-in desk in order for you to acquire a boarding card. Your passport is the only form of identification that will be accepted at the airport for international flights. If you're travelling with a baby, don't forget to carry their birth certificate, since it will be their sole form of identification.
